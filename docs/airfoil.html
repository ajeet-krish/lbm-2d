NACA 4-Digit Airfoil Analysis

The solver was applied to NACA 4-digit airfoils at Re = 1000 across angles of attack from 0 to 16 degrees. The airfoil is represented as a polygon obstacle using Bouzidi interpolated bounce-back for the curved surface. Chord = 160 cells at 800×300 resolution.

Important caveat: These LBM results are at Re = 1000 on a Cartesian grid, while experimental references (Ladson 1988, Sheldahl & Klimas 1981) are at Re > 105 with body-fitted grids. The purpose is to demonstrate that the lift curve slope matches thin-airfoil theory (2π per radian) within a few percent, not to match absolute Cl/Cd values at flight Reynolds numbers.

Validation

The linear lift slope dCl/dα from LBM is approximately 0.106 per degree, matching thin-airfoil theory (2π per radian = 0.109/deg) within 3%. This demonstrates that the solver captures the correct potential-flow physics despite the Cartesian grid.

Discussion

The airfoil case demonstrates the polygon obstacle pipeline: geometry generation via NACA 4-digit equations, point-in-polygon masking, and Bouzidi interpolated bounce-back on the sloped surfaces. The lift curve slope matches theory because the potential-flow pressure distribution around a thin airfoil is determined primarily by the camber line and angle of attack, not by viscous effects.

Future comparison: Run NACA 0012 at the same geometry in SU2 (Euler or laminar NS at Re = 1000) to compare LBM-Cartesian against SU2-unstructured for the same Reynolds number and grid-point budget.
