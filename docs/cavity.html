Lid-Driven Cavity Benchmark

The lid-driven cavity is a classic CFD validation case. A square cavity with three stationary walls and a moving top lid drives a recirculating flow. The flow transitions from a single primary vortex at low Re to developing secondary and tertiary corner eddies at higher Re. Centreline velocity profiles are the standard quantitative benchmark from Ghia, Ghia & Shin (1982).

Validation

At Re = 100, the solver matches Ghia's centerline u-velocity profile within 5-10% across the cavity height. The zero-crossing (vortex center) is captured at y/H ≈ 0.70 (Ghia: 0.734), and the peak return flow u/U ≈ -0.22 at y/H ≈ 0.45 (Ghia: -0.211 at y/H = 0.453).

Centerline Velocity Profiles: Lid-Driven Cavity vs Ghia 1982

Discussion

The cavity case validates the core solver's ability to handle recirculating flow with no-slip walls on all four boundaries. Unlike external flow cases, the cavity has no inflow or outflow; the flow is driven entirely by the moving lid boundary condition. This exercises the Zou/He equilibrium enforcement at the lid and the bounce-back on stationary walls.

Physics-Informed Neural Network

A parametric PINN surrogate learns the cavity flow field as a continuous function of position (x, y) and Reynolds number (Re), enabling real-time interpolation across the design space. The architecture uses Fourier feature embedding (m=128 random sinusoidal projections, σ=5.0) to break the spectral bias of tanh activations, followed by an 8-layer MLP (256 hidden units, 593K parameters). Trained on Re=100 and Re=400 simultaneously with a hybrid loss: PDE residual + sparse sensor data + boundary conditions.

Architecture

Component Detail
Fourier features m=128, σ=5.0 (frozen random sinusoids on x,y only)
MLP 8 layers × 256 hidden, tanh activation
Total parameters 593,489
Input (x, y, Ren) — 513 dimensions after Fourier lift
Output (u, v, p)
Training data Re=100 + Re=400, 3000 importance-sampled sensors each
Training time 177 min (Apple M5, MPS backend)

Side-by-side comparison of the C++ LBM baseline and the PINN surrogate at Re=100. The velocity field is well captured across the entire cavity, with the primary vortex structure and wall-bounded shear layers reproduced accurately.

Results: Re=400

At Re=400, the flow develops a stronger primary vortex and incipient corner eddies. The PINN captures the tighter velocity gradients near the walls and the shifted vortex center.
