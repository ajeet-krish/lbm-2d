Ahmed Body (2D Slice)

The Ahmed body is a simplified automotive bluff body with a slanted rear surface. The slant angle controls the base pressure drag: moderate angles (25–30 deg) produce the lowest drag due to longitudinal vortex structures that energise the near wake. This 2D slice captures the base pressure drag trend vs slant angle.

Important caveat: The real Ahmed body flow is 3D with strong longitudinal vortices at the slant. A 2D slice captures only the base pressure drag trend, not the characteristic Cd drop at 25 degrees. The 3D vortices require a spanwise grid dimension (D3Q19) not available in this 2D solver.

Discussion

The Ahmed body demonstrates the polygon obstacle pipeline with Bouzidi interpolated bounce-back on a slanted surface. The body geometry is defined by four vertices forming a quadrilateral with a slanted rear face. The point-in-polygon test with a 0.5-cell offset determines the interior, and the Bouzidi method computes the boundary distance along each lattice direction.

Real-world relevance: Automotive aerodynamics accounts for 15–20% of highway fuel consumption. The Ahmed body is the standard benchmark for wake flow control studies (active base bleed, boat-tailing, vortex generators). A future D3Q19 extension would capture the full 3D wake physics.
