Square Cylinder: Sharp-Edge Separation (ERCOFTAC 043)

Flow past a square cylinder is the canonical sharp-edge separation benchmark (ERCOFTAC case 043). Unlike the circular cylinder, separation is fixed at the sharp corners, producing a wider wake and higher drag. This tests the solver's ability to handle geometric singularities and fixed separation points.

Setup

Parameter Value
Grid 800 × 300
Cylinder side D 30 cells (NY/10)
Reynolds number 200
Inlet velocity uinflow = 0.1 lu/ts
Tau (relaxation parameter) 0.55
Number of steps 80,000
Reference length D = 30 cells (side length)
Collision MRT (d'Humieres 2002)
Boundary condition Bounce-back (sharp corners)
Lattice spacing / time step Δx = 1, Δt = 1

The square cylinder has higher Cd than the circular cylinder at the same Re due to the fixed separation at the sharp leading edges. The wider wake produces stronger vortex shedding and higher pressure drag.

Discussion

The square cylinder exercises the solver's handling of: (1) sharp geometric corners that fix the separation point, (2) bluff-body wake dynamics, and (3) periodic vortex shedding at moderate Re. Unlike the circular cylinder where separation moves with Re, the square cylinder separation is geometrically locked, providing a more predictable benchmark.
