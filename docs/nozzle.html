Converging-Diverging Nozzle: Bernoulli Validation

A smooth cosine-profile converging-diverging nozzle validates the solver's ability to handle internal flows with variable cross-section. The Bernoulli equation predicts velocity and pressure distributions for subsonic inviscid flow, providing an analytical benchmark. Smagorinsky LES activates automatically at high Re (tau < 0.55).

Bernoulli Theory

For subsonic inviscid flow, the Bernoulli equation gives:

v(x) = Q / A(x)

p(x) = p0 - 0.5 * rho * v(x)2

Where Q is the volume flow rate and A(x) is the local cross-sectional area. The nozzle wall profile follows a cosine curve: ywall(x) = (NY/2) * [1 - (1 - AR) * sin2(pi * x / L)].

High-Re cases (Re=500, 1000) use reduced inlet velocity (u_ref=0.05) and auto-LES to maintain stability. The throat velocity reaches 4x inlet velocity for AR=0.25, approaching the LBM compressibility limit.

Discussion

The CD nozzle tests internal flow acceleration/deceleration, pressure recovery, and wall boundary conditions. The smooth cosine profile avoids geometric singularities, making it ideal for Bouzidi interpolated bounce-back. The Bernoulli validation is exact for inviscid flow; viscous effects (boundary layers) cause slight deviations that decrease with increasing Re.
