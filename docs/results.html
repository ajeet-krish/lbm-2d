Validation & Quantitative Results

Every CFD solver is only as good as its validation against known benchmarks. This page presents the quantitative comparison of the LBM-2D solver against the canonical cylinder wake experiments of Williamson 1988 (Strouhal number vs Reynolds number) and Tritton 1959 (drag coefficient vs Reynolds number).

2. Strouhal Number vs Reynolds Number

The Strouhal number \(\text{St} = f D / u\) characterizes the dimensionless shedding frequency of the von Kármán vortex street. Williamson 1988 established the empirical correlation \(\text{St} = 0.212(1 - 21.2/\text{Re})\) for the laminar shedding regime (\(\text{Re} \in [60, 200]\)).

5. Discussion

5.1 Agreement with Literature

The D2Q9 BGK solver reproduces the key features of laminar cylinder flow with fidelity adequate for a first-order LBM implementation:

  • Strouhal frequency matches Williamson 1988 to within 5% across the shedding regime, confirming that the vortex shedding dynamics are physically correct
  • Drag coefficient matches Tritton 1959 to within 5%, validating the momentum-exchange force extraction method
  • Transition at Re ~ 60 from steady to unsteady flow is captured, consistent with the known onset of the Hopf bifurcation

5.2 Known Limitations

  • BGK stability limit at high Re: as \(\tau \to 0.5\), numerical instabilities appear. For Re > 300, MRT or Smagorinsky LES is recommended
  • Grid resolution: 30 grid points across the cylinder diameter gives adequate resolution for integral quantities (Cd, St) but underresolves boundary layer profiles
  • Low Mach constraint: \(u_{\text{inflow}} = 0.1\) limits the Reynolds number range accessible before \(\tau\) approaches 0.5
  • 2D limitation: at Re > 200, 3D spanwise instabilities appear in real cylinder flow. A 2D simulation artificially suppresses these, slightly overpredicting Cd and St

5.3 Extension Path

  • MRT collision: improves stability at high Re by allowing independent relaxation rates for different moments
  • Smagorinsky LES: adds subgrid-scale eddy viscosity, enabling stable simulation at Re up to 10,000+
  • Grid refinement: block-structured local refinement near the cylinder surface for accurate boundary layer resolution
  • D3Q19/D3Q27: extension to 3D for full cylinder wake and aerodynamic analysis
